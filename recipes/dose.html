Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish, known for its crispy texture and delicious taste. It's a type of thin, crepe-like pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). Dosa is often enjoyed as a breakfast item or a snack, served hot with a variety of accompaniments such as coconut chutney, sambar (a spicy lentil soup), and potato masala (a savory potato filling).

The process of making dosa involves soaking rice and urad dal, grinding them into a smooth batter, and allowing it to ferment overnight. This fermentation process gives dosa its characteristic tangy flavor and makes it light and crispy. With its versatility and delightful combination of flavors, dosa has become a beloved dish not just in India but worldwide.

Ingredient
  • Rice: 2 cups
  • Urad dal (black gram): 1 cup
  • Fenugreek seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: To taste
  • Oil or ghee: For cooking
  1. Prepare the Batter: Rinse and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for at least 4-6 hours. Grind the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth paste. Then grind the rice to a slightly coarse consistency. Combine both pastes and mix well.
  2. Ferment the Batter: Transfer the batter to a large bowl, add salt, and mix thoroughly. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight, until it doubles in volume and becomes slightly frothy.
  3. Prepare the Dosa Tawa: Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa (griddle) on medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  4. Cook the Dosa: Pour a ladleful of batter onto the hot tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crispy, then flip and cook the other side briefly.
  5. Serve the Dosa: Serve the hot, crispy dosa immediately with coconut chutney, sambar, and potato masala. Enjoy your homemade dosa!
